Adobe Inclusive Design Challenge - Apna Batuva

  • December 2016

Submitted as an individual contestant for the Inclusive Design Challenge, a one-day design competition sponsored by Adobe Systems, as a part of India HCI 2016, India's leading conference on human-computer interaction. This was selected amongst six contestants shortlisted for presentation in front of the panel of Adobe Design Competition Judges.

Design Challenge Details

Primary Design Objective :
To design a payment system for elderly emergent users who intend to buy daily vegetables from their regular street vendors. How might we enable these emergent users to perform a cashless transaction?

Primary user personae/ demographics :
- Indian elderly users
- age 55 to 75 years
- with very minimum or no knowledge of modern technology.
- may know or own smartphones but uncomfortable with the world of cashless transactions, unaware of concepts like e-banking, OTP, IVR or QR codes.
- have displayed an aversion to the adoption of technology powered lifestyle because of the complexity it comes with.

This system could be powered by the convenience of technology but centered around usage pattern of elderly non-technical users.

This design objective bears increased relevance as India aims to move towards a cashless economy.
